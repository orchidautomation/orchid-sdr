# Research Checks

Run these checks before returning a research brief or using research to write copy.

## Identity Coherence

Verify:

- the person identity is consistent across the signal and profile
- the company identity is consistent across the signal, profile, and company page
- the company domain and brand actually match the prospect you think you are looking at

If identity is fuzzy, lower confidence and say so directly.

## Evidence Quality

Prefer evidence that is:

- public
- recent
- attributable
- directly observed

Treat these as strongest:

- the source post itself
- the profile page
- the company site
- recent company news

Treat broad web results as supporting context, not primary truth.

## Fact vs Inference

Separate:

- observed facts
- plausible inferences
- open questions

If something is inferred, mark it as inferred.
Do not smuggle hypotheses into the brief as facts.

## Signal Strength

Ask:

- is the source post actually meaningful?
- does the profile support the interpretation?
- does the company context make the signal commercially relevant?
- does recent news change the timing?

Topical adjacency is not enough.

## Evidence Selection

Keep only the strongest 3 to 5 evidence points.

Each evidence point should help answer one of these:

- why this person matters
- why this company matters
- why now might matter
- why the product could be relevant

Discard generic filler.

## Confidence

Raise confidence when:

- identity is clean
- evidence is direct
- multiple sources agree
- the signal is commercially meaningful

Lower confidence when:

- evidence is thin
- evidence is contradictory
- identity is ambiguous
- timing is speculative
- the company context is missing

## Prohibited Output

Do not:

- pad the brief with obvious statements
- restate the ICP in abstract terms
- pretend the research is stronger than it is
- write a mini sales pitch instead of a research artifact
