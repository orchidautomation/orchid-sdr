# Common Room Demo ICP

This demo qualifies opted-in website or LinkedIn form leads for a Common Room-style GTM motion.

## Best-fit accounts

Prioritize B2B companies where pipeline generation depends on identifying the right account/person at the right time:

- SaaS, infrastructure, AI, data, devtools, security, fintech, support, collaboration, or vertical B2B platforms.
- 50 to 2,000 employees, especially companies with active sales, SDR, growth, RevOps, or marketing teams.
- High-value B2B sales motion where timing, intent, product usage, hiring, open-source activity, community activity, or account expansion signals matter.
- Evidence of fragmented GTM operations: multiple channels, product-led usage, Slack/community touchpoints, outbound experiments, CRM hygiene problems, or manual account research.
- Publicly discusses AI SDRs, outbound reliability, buyer signals, enrichment, routing, prioritization, speed-to-lead, or pipeline quality.

## Ideal buyers

Rank buyers in this order:

1. CRO, VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of Growth, Head of Demand Gen, or GTM leader.
2. RevOps, Sales Ops, Marketing Ops, Growth Ops, or GTM Engineering owner.
3. Founder/CEO at a fast-moving B2B company with a direct pipeline problem.
4. SDR/BDR leader responsible for prospecting quality and rep productivity.

## Strong buying signals

Treat these as qualified evidence:

- A form-fill mentions AI SDR reliability, Clay-style research, enrichment, outbound experiments, GTM engineering, routing, or CRM state.
- The company is hiring GTM, RevOps, sales development, growth, demand generation, or sales engineering roles.
- The company sells to B2B accounts where account context and timing matter.
- The company has many possible buyer/user signals: product usage, community, docs, GitHub, Slack, support channels, job changes, website visits, or content engagement.
- The lead asks for a workflow, benchmark, demo, playbook, or example instead of generic AI curiosity.

## Disqualifiers

Disqualify or mark `needs_review` when:

- The account is consumer-only, local services, pure ecommerce, or not running a B2B GTM motion.
- The person has no plausible responsibility for pipeline, growth, revenue, customer acquisition, or operations.
- The signal is too thin to explain why outreach is relevant.
- The payload lacks consent or explicitly says do not contact.
- Research contradicts the claimed company/domain/title.

## Qualification labels

Use `qualified` when there is a clear GTM signal, plausible buyer, and supporting public evidence.

Use `needs_review` when the company fits but the person, timing, consent, or research evidence is incomplete.

Use `disqualified` when the account does not match the GTM buyer-intelligence market or contact would be unsafe.

## Evidence standard

Prefer the form payload first, then Trellis research tools. Cite public sources in the research brief. Do not invent customers, funding, employee counts, tech stack, or private CRM facts.
