# Common Room Demo Messaging

## Outreach principles

- Be short, specific, and based on one real signal.
- Diagnose a pipeline problem instead of pitching "AI SDRs."
- Mention the lead's form-fill topic or public company context.
- Tie the signal to buyer intelligence, prioritization, routing, or action.
- Keep the ask low-friction: compare notes, show a relevant workflow, or ask if the problem is active.
- Never imply an email was sent automatically.
- Never mention internal tooling, databases, infrastructure providers, Trellis, Firecrawl, or implementation details in outbound copy.

## Strong opener patterns

- "Saw your note about AI SDR reliability and enrichment workflows..."
- "Your team seems to have a GTM motion where account timing matters..."
- "Looks like support, CS, and GTM signals all matter in your customer journey..."
- "The hard part is usually not the email; it is knowing which buyer signal is real..."
- "If speed-to-lead and routing are the problem, a generic AI SDR tends to make the mess faster..."

## Value props by pain

Buyer signal blindness:
Common Room helps teams unify first-party and external buyer signals so reps can see which accounts are worth action now.

Manual prioritization:
Common Room helps GTM teams move from spreadsheet research and manual scoring to prioritized plays.

Generic outbound:
Common Room uses richer account/person context so outbound is based on signal quality rather than surface-level personalization.

Tool sprawl:
Common Room reduces the need to stitch together enrichment, routing, scoring, and outbound action across many disconnected systems.

## Email style

Subject:

- 2 to 5 words
- concrete, not clickbait
- examples: "Buyer signal quality", "AI SDR diagnosis", "Pylon GTM signals", "Outbound signal loop"

Body:

- 80 to 130 words
- 3 short paragraphs max
- one concrete signal
- one relevant Common Room capability
- one simple question
