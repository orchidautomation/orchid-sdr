# Spring Health Reference

Spring Health is a B2B mental health benefits platform. Employers and health plans buy on behalf of employees, members, and dependents.

## Products

- SpringLife: member-facing experience for employees and dependents.
- SpringWorks for Employers: HR/admin tools, reporting, and ROI dashboards.
- SpringWorks for Health Plans: health-plan version of SpringWorks.
- Compass: clinical provider system of record.

## Buyer Personas

- HR, Benefits, or Total Rewards leader: primary buyer and day-to-day champion.
- CHRO: executive sponsor for workforce wellbeing and cost trend.
- CFO or Benefits Finance: economic buyer for ROI, claims impact, and renewal justification.
- Internal Comms or People Ops: drives benefit awareness and launch execution.
- Health-plan buyer: behavioral health, network, clinical, and plan finance leaders.

## What Good Looks Like

| Signal | Healthy | Watch | At risk |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Member registration rate | >35% | 20-35% | <20% |
| Utilization rate | >12% | 8-12% | <8% |
| HR admin logins | weekly+ | monthly | <quarterly |
| Outcomes report delivered | last 90 days | last 180 days | >180 days |
| Internal comms launches | at least quarterly | 1-2/year | none |
| Executive sponsor engagement | last 90 days | last 180 days | unreachable or changed roles |

Do not treat generic DAU or MAU as the customer health metric. Translate member app activity into SpringLife activity, then pair it with registration rate and utilization rate.

## Renewal Mechanics

Employer contracts are usually annual or multi-year and aligned to a benefits plan year. Common cutovers are January 1 and July 1. Open enrollment is the leverage window because member communications and benefit education drive the next year of usage.

Health-plan contracts often behave differently: risk may show up as scope reduction, carve-out, or delayed expansion instead of full cancellation.

## Common Churn Drivers

1. Low utilization creates an ROI challenge.
2. Eligibility or HRIS integration friction blocks registration.
3. Champion change causes the successor to reassess.
4. Member experience escalation reaches senior leadership.
5. Reporting gaps erode trust before renewal.
6. Competitive carve-out pressure reframes the account.

Every recommended action should land with a named buyer persona. A plan that depends on the CFO when only a Benefits Analyst is engaged is incomplete.
