# Ideal Customer Profile

## Product Fit Summary

PlayKit fits teams that are already committed to Clay, or are close enough to adoption that Clay workflow quality materially affects revenue operations.

The strongest fit is not "anyone doing outbound." The strongest fit is operators who:

- build or maintain Clay workflows regularly
- care about credit efficiency and workflow correctness
- need production-ready work product, not generic advice
- use AI clients like Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, n8n, or custom agents

## Primary Customer Segments

### 1. GTM Engineers

Best fit:

- dedicated GTM engineers or technical growth operators
- GTM engineering leaders and builders explicitly using GTM as a systems or infrastructure discipline
- individuals responsible for Clay architecture, provider selection, waterfalls, and automation quality
- teams moving fast enough that wrong table design or bad enrichments have real cost

Why they buy:

- they want expert-quality Clay architectures without years of trial and error
- they need fast answers on providers, workflows, prompts, and table design
- they want to stop wasting credits on poorly structured builds

### 2. RevOps and Growth Ops Teams

Best fit:

- RevOps teams implementing Clay for outbound, ABM, enrichment, routing, or signal-based workflows
- operators who need Clay done correctly the first time
- teams that need repeatable process, documentation, and lower rework

Why they buy:

- they are overwhelmed by Clay's surface area
- they need confident decisions on table structure, waterfalls, and prompt design
- they want fewer rebuilds and fewer "we should have structured this differently" mistakes

### 3. Agencies and Service Providers

Best fit:

- outbound agencies, Clay consultancies, GTM engineering shops, and RevOps service firms
- firms with junior operators delivering work under senior review
- teams managing multiple client workspaces and repeated implementation patterns

Why they buy:

- they need consistent output quality across clients
- they want junior team members to produce senior-level work
- they benefit directly from reusable architectures, audits, and shareable docs

### 4. Agent Builders and Technical Product Teams

Best fit:

- teams embedding Clay expertise into their own agents, copilots, or internal tools
- builders using MCP-compatible clients and orchestration stacks
- teams that do not want to maintain a large, fast-changing Clay knowledge base internally

Why they buy:

- PlayKit is composable with MCP clients
- they can add Clay expertise to an existing agent stack without building that layer themselves
- they want workflow design and provider reasoning to be queryable infrastructure

## Company Profile

Best-fit company traits:

- B2B go-to-market motion
- active outbound, ABM, enrichment, or signal-monitoring workflows
- already using Clay, evaluating Clay, or clearly planning Clay adoption
- enough workflow volume that credit optimization matters
- enough complexity that operator confidence matters more than generic AI answers

Common company types:

- SaaS companies with technical GTM teams
- agencies building client outbound systems
- RevOps-heavy B2B teams with complex enrichment and routing workflows
- product or platform teams building AI-assisted GTM infrastructure

## Buyer Personas

Primary buyers:

- GTM Engineer
- Head of RevOps
- RevOps Manager
- Growth Operations Lead
- Agency Founder
- Agency Delivery Lead
- Product Engineer building GTM agents

Secondary influencers:

- VP Sales or VP Growth
- founder-led operator at an early-stage B2B company
- technical consultant running Clay for clients

## Core Pains

PlayKit should be sold into these pains:

- "I think Clay can do that, but I'm not sure how to build it correctly."
- too many docs, tutorials, Slack threads, and outdated examples
- uncertainty around table architecture and multi-table design
- uncertainty around provider waterfalls, BYOA, and enrichment tradeoffs
- credit burn from bad workflow design
- lack of confidence in Claygent and AI Action prompts
- inconsistent implementation quality across operators or clients
- slow time-to-value when turning an idea into a deployable Clay workflow

## Trigger Events

Strong timing triggers:

- team just adopted Clay or is onboarding new Clay users
- operator is building a new outbound, ABM, or signal-based play
- team is scaling Clay usage and credit waste becomes visible
- agency is standardizing delivery across multiple clients
- company is building AI agents that need to reason about Clay
- team needs webhook tables, table audits, or shareable table docs

## Positive Buying Signals

Good signals in outreach or qualification:

- mentions Clay, Claygent, waterfalls, enrichments, or provider comparison
- mentions GTM, GTM engineering, GTM systems, or technical go-to-market infrastructure
- mentions Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, or agents in the context of GTM, outbound, enrichment, or workflow design
- mentions outbound systems, ABM workflows, or signal-based GTM plays
- complaints about wasted credits or bad table structure
- uses Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, n8n, or custom agents in ops workflows
- hires for GTM engineering, RevOps engineering, or technical growth roles
- runs an agency or consultancy delivering Clay implementations

## Negative Signals

Poor-fit traits:

- no Clay usage and no clear plan to use Clay
- wants simple email blasting rather than workflow design quality
- needs fully managed lead generation rather than operator enablement
- no technical or process owner for GTM systems
- extremely low workflow volume where credit optimization does not matter
- unwilling to work in MCP or agent-enabled tooling

## Messaging Angles That Should Land

- confidence, not doc summaries
- production-ready work product, not generic suggestions
- 60-90% credit savings through better workflow design
- faster time from question to deployable table or workflow
- embed Clay expertise in any MCP-compatible agent
- consistent delivery quality across operators and clients

## Land And Expand Path

Likely expansion path:

- start with workflow design and provider comparison
- expand into prompt generation and outreach support
- expand into webhook tables, audits, and shareable documentation
- expand from one operator to a team or agency-wide operating layer

## Pricing Fit

- Free / Builder: solo GTM engineers, early evaluators, and individual operators
- Pro: serious in-house operators using PlayKit weekly
- Agency: client-service teams running repeatable Clay operations at scale
- Enterprise: larger teams needing SLAs, custom integrations, and broad deployment
