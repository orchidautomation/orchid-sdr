# The AI SDR Industry Is Broken

The AI SDR market is full of software that is too expensive, too opaque, and too shallow for what companies actually need.

That is the blunt version.

The polished version is that the category is still being built around the wrong abstraction.

Most current products behave as if the core problem is:

“How do we automate outbound email?”

That is not the real problem.

The real problem is:

- finding credible signals
- understanding whether those signals matter
- knowing whether the person is actually a fit
- knowing whether the company is actually a fit
- grounding the outreach in real research
- and keeping the whole system inspectable

The hard part is not the send button.

The hard part is the reasoning system behind the send button.

That is where most of the category is weak.

A lot of AI SDR tools today feel like this:

- a sequence builder
- a fragile enrichment flow
- a black-box scoring layer
- generic AI copy
- an admin dashboard
- and a large price tag

What is missing is durable judgment.

That shows up in three ways.

## 1. Most AI SDRs Over-Invest In Surfaces

There is usually a lot of chrome:

- dashboards
- tabs
- workflow builders
- configuration screens
- attribution views

But when you ask the basic question — why did the system decide this lead was worth contacting? — the answer is often weak.

Maybe there is a score.
Maybe there is a reason string.
Maybe there is a generated explanation.

But often there is not a trustworthy, inspectable path from source signal to final decision.

That means the product looks more mature than it actually is.

## 2. Most AI SDRs Under-Invest In State

Outbound is not one prompt.

It is a stateful system.

You need to know:

- what the system already saw
- what it already searched
- what signals it already tried
- what it already sent
- what thread a reply belongs to
- why a prospect was paused
- which CRM records already exist

If you do not model that explicitly, the system becomes brittle fast.

This is why so many “AI SDRs” feel okay in a demo and messy in production.

They are really running a collection of one-off decisions without a durable operating model underneath.

## 3. Most AI SDRs Are Too Closed To Improve Cleanly

If you want to change:

- the signal source
- the research provider
- the qualification logic
- the send provider
- the CRM target
- the model

you should not need to replace the product.

But in a lot of current tools, that is exactly what happens.

The more proprietary the system, the more every useful change turns into:

- a support request
- a professional services engagement
- or a hard platform limitation

That is not intelligence.

That is packaging.

## What The Category Should Look Like Instead

An AI SDR should be AI-native software.

That means:

- durable workflows
- explicit state
- composable tools
- inspectable persistence
- deterministic business integrations
- model flexibility

In other words, it should look more like a control plane than a chatbot.

It should be able to:

1. wake up on a schedule
2. decide what to search for from the ICP
3. monitor public signals
4. normalize those signals into one contract
5. research the post, person, company, and news
6. qualify against real product context
7. write the reasoning down
8. sync the right records
9. send or hand off only when appropriate

That is the shape of a serious system.

And once you build it that way, the category starts looking very different.

The interesting companies will not be the ones with the prettiest sequence builder.

They will be the ones with:

- the best signal loops
- the best qualification systems
- the best memory
- the best operator surfaces
- the best composability story

That is the category I think should exist.

And it is why I think the current one is still broken.
